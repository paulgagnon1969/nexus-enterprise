## ARTICLE 1 — DEFINITIONS

### 1.1 "Confidential Information"
Any and all information, in any form or medium (whether written, oral, electronic, visual, machine-readable, or otherwise), that is disclosed by or on behalf of Nexus to Recipient, or that Recipient obtains, observes, or otherwise acquires access to in connection with this Agreement, including without limitation:

(a) **Technical Information**: Software source code, object code, algorithms, data structures, architectures, system designs, API specifications, database schemas, data models, integration methodologies, deployment configurations, infrastructure topology, security protocols, and technical documentation;

(b) **Business Information**: Business plans, strategies, financial data, pricing models, cost structures, customer lists, vendor relationships, marketing strategies, competitive analyses, and operational methodologies;

(c) **Product Information**: Product roadmaps, feature specifications, user interface designs, user experience research, product analytics, performance benchmarks, and development timelines;

(d) **Proprietary Software**: The Nexus software platform in its entirety, including all modules, sub-systems, microservices, worker processes, and supporting infrastructure, collectively known as "Nexus," "NCC" (Nexus Command Center), or any successor branding;

(e) **Module Architecture**: The design, structure, interdependencies, data flow, and integration patterns of all complementary modules within the Nexus ecosystem, including but not limited to estimating, scheduling, financial management, workforce management, document management, project management, client collaboration, and compliance modules;

(f) **Marketplace Intellectual Property**: The design, methodology, algorithms, business logic, curation systems, sovereign registration processes, asset management workflows, billing integration, and self-service capabilities of the Nexus Marketplace platform;

(g) **Documents**: All documents accessed through the Nexus document system, including Competitive Advantage Modules (CAMs), Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs), technical manuals, architecture documents, and any document bearing a "CONFIDENTIAL," "PROPRIETARY," or similar designation;

(h) **Derivative Information**: Any information derived from, based upon, or created using any of the foregoing, including analyses, compilations, studies, notes, interpretations, or other documents or materials prepared by Recipient that contain, reflect, or are based on Confidential Information.

### 1.2 "Protected Documents"
Any document, manual, report, specification, or digital content accessed through the Nexus document sharing system that is subject to access controls, authentication requirements, watermarking, serialization, or encryption protections.

### 1.3 "Proprietary Software"
The complete Nexus software platform, including all current and future versions, modules, extensions, integrations, and related technologies, whether in source code, object code, documentation, or any other form.

### 1.4 "Complementary Module Architecture"
The unique design philosophy, integration methodology, and interoperability framework through which independent functional modules within the Nexus ecosystem operate together as a unified platform, including the specific patterns of data exchange, shared services, event-driven communication, and user experience continuity across modules.

### 1.5 "Marketplace Curation"
The proprietary system, algorithms, business rules, and methodologies by which the Nexus Marketplace enables sovereign entity registration, asset management, service provider matching, maintenance scheduling, billing integration, and self-service administration for individuals and organizations.

### 1.6 "Intellectual Property" or "IP"
All patents, patent applications, copyrights, trademarks, service marks, trade names, trade secrets, know-how, inventions, processes, techniques, methodologies, and any other proprietary rights of Nexus, whether registered or unregistered, and whether existing now or developed in the future.

### 1.7 "Forensic Serial Number"
The unique alphanumeric identifier assigned to each instance of document access or download, embedded in document metadata, watermarks, and access logs for the purpose of tracking and identifying the origin of any unauthorized disclosure.

## ARTICLE 2 — SCOPE OF CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION

### 2.1 Presumption of Confidentiality
All information disclosed by Nexus to Recipient in connection with this Agreement shall be presumed Confidential Information unless expressly designated otherwise in writing by an authorized officer of Nexus.

### 2.2 Exclusions
Confidential Information does not include information that Recipient can demonstrate by clear and convincing documentary evidence:

(a) Was in the public domain at the time of disclosure through no fault of Recipient;

(b) Became part of the public domain after disclosure through no fault, act, or omission of Recipient;

(c) Was lawfully in Recipient's possession prior to disclosure, without any obligation of confidentiality, as evidenced by written records predating the Effective Date;

(d) Was independently developed by Recipient without reference to, use of, or reliance upon any Confidential Information, as evidenced by contemporaneous written records; or

(e) Was received by Recipient from a third party who was not, to Recipient's knowledge, under any obligation of confidentiality to Nexus.

### 2.3 Burden of Proof
The burden of establishing any exclusion under Section 2.2 rests solely with Recipient.

### 2.4 Compelled Disclosure
If Recipient becomes legally compelled to disclose any Confidential Information by a court of competent jurisdiction, governmental authority, or regulatory body, Recipient shall:

(a) Provide Nexus with prompt written notice (and in any event no less than five (5) business days prior to disclosure) to allow Nexus to seek a protective order or other appropriate remedy;

(b) Cooperate fully with Nexus in seeking such protective order;

(c) Disclose only the minimum portion of Confidential Information legally required; and

(d) Use commercially reasonable efforts to obtain reliable assurance that confidential treatment will be accorded to any Confidential Information so disclosed.

## ARTICLE 3 — OBLIGATIONS OF RECEIVING PARTY

### 3.1 Non-Disclosure
Recipient shall not disclose, publish, disseminate, or otherwise make available any Confidential Information to any third party without the prior written consent of Nexus. This obligation extends to all forms of disclosure, including verbal, written, electronic, visual, and any form of communication or transfer.

### 3.2 Non-Use
Recipient shall use Confidential Information solely for the purpose for which it was disclosed (the "Permitted Purpose"), which is limited to evaluating, reviewing, or considering a potential business relationship with Nexus. Recipient shall not use any Confidential Information for any other purpose, including without limitation:

(a) Developing, designing, or engineering any competing product, service, or technology;

(b) Improving, enhancing, or modifying any existing product, service, or technology of Recipient or any third party;

(c) Soliciting or recruiting any employee, contractor, or business partner of Nexus;

(d) Gaining any commercial, competitive, or strategic advantage.

### 3.3 Standard of Care
Recipient shall protect Confidential Information using at least the same degree of care as Recipient uses to protect its own most sensitive confidential information, but in no event less than a reasonable degree of care. Recipient shall implement and maintain appropriate technical, administrative, and physical safeguards to prevent unauthorized access, use, or disclosure.

### 3.4 Need-to-Know Restriction
Recipient shall limit access to Confidential Information to those of its employees, agents, or advisors who: (a) have a genuine need to know for the Permitted Purpose; (b) are informed of the confidential nature of the information; and (c) are bound by written confidentiality obligations no less restrictive than those contained herein.

### 3.5 No Copies
Recipient shall not copy, reproduce, photograph, screenshot, screen-record, transcribe, or otherwise duplicate any Confidential Information or Protected Documents, except as expressly authorized by Nexus in writing.

### 3.6 No Circumvention of Protections
Recipient shall not attempt to circumvent, disable, defeat, or bypass any technical protection measures applied to Protected Documents, including but not limited to: digital watermarks, serial number tracking, encryption, password protection, copy prevention mechanisms, print restrictions, or access controls.

## ARTICLE 4 — REVERSE ENGINEERING PRECLUSION

### 4.1 Absolute Prohibition
Recipient shall NOT, under any circumstances, directly or indirectly:

(a) **Reverse engineer** any Proprietary Software, module, component, algorithm, data structure, or system design of Nexus;

(b) **Decompile** any compiled or object code belonging to or used by Nexus;

(c) **Disassemble** any software, firmware, or hardware configuration of Nexus;

(d) **Deconstruct** the architecture, design patterns, integration methodology, or interoperability framework of any Nexus module or system;

(e) **Extract** algorithms, business logic, data models, or processing methodologies from any Nexus software or documentation;

(f) **Recreate** or attempt to recreate any functionality, feature, workflow, or user experience of any Nexus module through observation, documentation, or analysis of Protected Documents or demonstrations;

(g) **Analyze** Protected Documents or any other Confidential Information for the purpose of creating, developing, or assisting in the development of any product, service, or technology that is similar to, competes with, or substitutes for any Nexus product or service.

### 4.2 Scope of Prohibition
The reverse engineering prohibition extends to:

(a) All current and future versions of the Nexus platform;

(b) All modules, including but not limited to: estimating, scheduling (including Gantt functionality), daily logs, time and payroll, invoicing, financial management, document management, project management, client collaboration (Collaborator Technology), workforce management, compliance, and marketplace;

(c) The unique integration patterns and data flows between complementary modules;

(d) The marketplace curation system and sovereign registration methodology;

(e) Any AI/ML models, OCR systems, or automation workflows incorporated into the platform;

(f) The security architecture, authentication systems, and access control mechanisms.

### 4.3 Derivative Works
Recipient shall not create, commission, or assist in the creation of any derivative work based on, inspired by, or substantially similar to any aspect of Nexus's Confidential Information, Proprietary Software, Complementary Module Architecture, or Marketplace Curation.

### 4.4 Personnel Restriction
Recipient shall not engage, employ, or contract with any individual for the purpose of reverse engineering, analyzing, deconstructing, or recreating any aspect of Nexus's technology, whether or not such individual has direct access to Nexus's Confidential Information.

Recipient acknowledges and consents that all access to Protected Documents is tracked, logged, and recorded. Each access session generates a unique Forensic Serial Number that is:

### 9.3 Cooperation in Investigations

Any attempt to do so shall constitute a material breach of this Agreement and shall give rise to the presumption that Recipient has engaged in unauthorized disclosure.

## ARTICLE 10 — REMEDIES AND DAMAGES

### 10.1 Acknowledgment of Harm
Recipient acknowledges and agrees that:

(a) Nexus's Confidential Information, Intellectual Property, and trade secrets have been developed through substantial investment of time, capital, and effort;

(b) Unauthorized disclosure, use, or reverse engineering of Confidential Information would cause irreparable harm to Nexus that cannot be adequately compensated by monetary damages alone;

(c) The actual damages resulting from a breach of this Agreement may be difficult or impossible to calculate with precision;

(d) The remedies set forth in this Article 10 are reasonable in light of the anticipated harm and do not constitute a penalty.

### 10.2 Injunctive Relief
In the event of any actual or threatened breach of this Agreement, Nexus shall be entitled to seek immediate injunctive relief (including temporary restraining orders, preliminary injunctions, and permanent injunctions) from any court of competent jurisdiction, without the necessity of posting a bond or proving actual damages. Recipient hereby waives any requirement for Nexus to post a bond in connection with seeking injunctive relief.

### 10.3 Liquidated Damages
Because the actual damages from unauthorized disclosure or misuse of Confidential Information are difficult to determine, the Parties agree to the following liquidated damages, which represent a genuine pre-estimate of the probable loss:

(a) **Per-Incident Unauthorized Disclosure**: Fifty Thousand Dollars ($50,000) per incident of unauthorized disclosure of Confidential Information to any unauthorized third party;

(b) **Per-Document Breach**: Twenty-Five Thousand Dollars ($25,000) per Protected Document that is shared, copied, distributed, or made available in violation of this Agreement;

(c) **Reverse Engineering Violation**: Five Hundred Thousand Dollars ($500,000) per occurrence of reverse engineering, decompilation, disassembly, or deconstruction of any Nexus technology in violation of Article 4;

(d) **Marketplace IP Misappropriation**: Two Hundred Fifty Thousand Dollars ($250,000) per instance of misappropriation, replication, or unauthorized use of Nexus Marketplace intellectual property;

(e) **Circumvention of Protections**: One Hundred Thousand Dollars ($100,000) per instance of circumventing, tampering with, or defeating any technical protection measure in violation of Section 3.6;

(f) **Accumulation**: Liquidated damages under this Section are cumulative and may be assessed for each separate violation. A single course of conduct may give rise to multiple violations.

### 10.4 Actual Damages
The liquidated damages set forth in Section 10.3 are in addition to (not in lieu of) any actual, consequential, incidental, or special damages to which Nexus may be entitled. If Nexus's actual damages exceed the applicable liquidated damages amount, Nexus reserves the right to pursue the full extent of actual damages.

### 10.5 Disgorgement of Profits
In the event Recipient or any third party derives any revenue, profit, or commercial benefit from the unauthorized use of Confidential Information, Nexus shall be entitled to disgorgement of all such profits in addition to any other damages.

### 10.6 Attorney's Fees and Costs
In any action to enforce this Agreement or arising from a breach hereof, the prevailing party shall be entitled to recover its reasonable attorney's fees, expert witness fees, court costs, and other litigation expenses from the non-prevailing party.

### 10.7 Criminal Referral
Nexus reserves the right to refer any suspected misappropriation of trade secrets to federal and state law enforcement authorities for criminal prosecution under the Economic Espionage Act (18 U.S.C. §§ 1831–1839), the Defend Trade Secrets Act (18 U.S.C. § 1836 et seq.), and/or applicable Texas criminal statutes.

## ARTICLE 11 — TERM AND TERMINATION

### 11.1 Term
This Agreement shall remain in effect for a period of five (5) years from the Effective Date, unless terminated earlier in accordance with this Section.

### 11.2 Termination
Either Party may terminate this Agreement upon thirty (30) days' written notice to the other Party. Nexus may terminate this Agreement immediately upon notice if Recipient breaches any provision hereof.

### 11.3 Survival
The obligations of confidentiality, non-disclosure, non-use, and reverse engineering preclusion shall survive the termination or expiration of this Agreement for a period of ten (10) years following termination, or for so long as the Confidential Information qualifies as a trade secret under applicable law, whichever period is longer.

### 11.4 Post-Termination Obligations
Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement, Recipient shall immediately:

(a) Cease all use of Confidential Information;

(b) Return to Nexus or destroy all copies of Confidential Information in any form;

(c) Permanently delete all electronic copies from all devices, systems, and storage media;

(d) Certify in writing, signed by an authorized representative of Recipient, that all Confidential Information has been returned or destroyed.
