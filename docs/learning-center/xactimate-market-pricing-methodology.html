Xactimate Market Pricing Methodology

Understanding Verisk's Data-Driven Approach to Fair Market Value Estimation

Verisk's Xactimate is widely regarded in the property insurance and restoration industries as a reliable tool for generating estimates that reflect fair market value (often termed "market price"). This stems from its data-driven methodology, which aggregates real-world pricing information to produce localized, representative costs for materials, labor, and equipment.

Key Justifications

  • Market-Based Data Aggregation

    Xactimate pricing is derived from millions of data points collected monthly, including surveys of contractors/suppliers, direct supplier feeds, industry participants, and anonymized completed estimates/bids submitted by users. Verisk analyzes these to identify market consensus via proprietary cluster analysis (developed by economists), filtering anomalies to report a representative "most common" or median-like price within observed ranges. This approach aims to capture genuine market conditions rather than arbitrary or biased inputs.

  • Transparent and Defensible Pricing

    Verisk explicitly describes its role as reporting a market price based on recent transactions and surveys, providing a baseline for estimators to start from and adjust as needed for specific jobs. Primary sources encompass diverse stakeholders (contractors, subcontractors, material providers, and Xactimate users) to avoid undue influence from any single party. Published prices reflect historical and current market realities across 460+ localized regions in the US and Canada.

  • Industry Recognition as a Fair Reference

    Xactimate is the dominant standard for property claims estimating, used by insurers, adjusters, and contractors to promote consistency and defensibility in settlements. While not a rigid "ceiling" or contractual mandate (Verisk clarifies it's a reference tool, not the definitive standard), it serves as strong evidence of fair market value when supported by the data methodology.

Third-Party Verifications and Citations

  • Verisk's official Pricing Research Methodology document outlines the process: surveys, estimate transactions, and analysis to report market prices reflecting the most common recently submitted costs.
  • Summaries from industry sources (e.g., Property Insurance Coverage Law Blog and restoration publications) confirm Xactimate reports a "market price" from real submissions and surveys, serving as a valuable, transparent baseline for fair estimates.
