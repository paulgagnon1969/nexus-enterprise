## Executive Summaries

### Chapter 1 — Executive Summary
*High-conviction investment in a scalable, mixed-use development on 174.61 acres in New Braunfels, TX ETJ.*

Phase 1 targets garden-style multifamily (128 units across 5 buildings) with projected equity IRR of 18–24% (base case 21%) over a 5–7 year hold. The $4.2M equity raise is paired with $3.6M construction debt for a total Phase 1 cost of $7.8M under Nexus CMS development pricing. The team brings 250+ years of combined experience and NASCLA certification across 18+ states.

→ [Full Section](./ELM-CREEK-PROSPECTUS-MANUAL.md#section-1--chapter-1-executive-summary-rev-8)

### Chapter 2 — Project Overview & Opportunity
*Two fully owned parcels totaling 174.61 acres with creek frontage, I-35 access, and agricultural exemptions.*

The site at 535–539 Elm Creek Rd sits in the Comal County ETJ with deed recorded since 2010. Phase 1 develops 128 garden-style units (1 Style B building at 32 units + 4 Style A buildings at 24 units each). The New Braunfels corridor sees +2.8% annual population growth with slowing new supply, and comparable rents average $1,440/mo with high occupancy.

→ [Full Section](./ELM-CREEK-PROSPECTUS-MANUAL.md#section-2--chapter-2-project-overview--opportunity-rev-9)

### Chapter 3 — Financial Projections & Use of Funds
*$7.8M total cost with ~35% savings through Nexus CMS vertical integration.*

Capital stack: $4.2M equity + $3.6M debt (65% LTC). The phased build/refi model constructs 5 buildings sequentially (6 mo build + 2 mo lease-up each), refinancing at stabilized appraised values. Cumulative refi profits of ~$5.52M over 32 months provide internal capital for Phase 2 with minimal/no new equity. Per-unit hard costs post-savings: ~$61k vs. FMV comps of $180k–$220k.

→ [Full Section](./ELM-CREEK-PROSPECTUS-MANUAL.md#section-3--chapter-3-financial-projections--use-of-funds-rev-7)

### Chapter 4 — Timeline, Risks & Mitigation
*32-month phased timeline with NCC real-time transparency for all stakeholders.*

Five key risks addressed: entitlement delays, cost overruns, lease-up delays, interest rate shifts, and sequential phasing cascades. Each is mitigated through a combination of NCC's PETL daily tracking, conservative assumptions (94% occupancy, 6.5% cap rate), GMP contracts, and phased refi strategy that spreads exposure.

→ [Full Section](./ELM-CREEK-PROSPECTUS-MANUAL.md#section-4--chapter-4-timeline-risks--mitigation-rev-7)

### Chapter 5 — Development & Construction Team
*Vertically integrated execution under Nexus Fortified Structures, LLC with proprietary NCC software.*

The team operates as sponsor, developer, and GC — eliminating third-party markups. GC O&P (typically 20%) is waived. NASCLA certification enables licensing in 18+ states. The NCC platform provides investor-facing dashboards with PETL scheduling, weekly reconciliation, and variance flagging at the room/unit/building level.

→ [Full Section](./ELM-CREEK-PROSPECTUS-MANUAL.md#section-5--chapter-5-development--construction-team-executive-overview-rev-7)

### Chapter 6 — Investment Opportunity & Terms
*Seeking $500K–$3M per accredited investor; mezzanine equity with 8% preferred return.*

Investors benefit from a de-risked entry: $3.67M FMV land contributed as equity (no cash purchase), deferred profits LOI providing ~35% cost savings, and a phased refi model that can return principal as early as Year 1–2 per building. Two parties are in pre-LOI discussions. Terms include 8% preferred return with promote after 12% IRR.

→ [Full Section](./ELM-CREEK-PROSPECTUS-MANUAL.md#section-6--chapter-6-investment-opportunity--terms-rev-2)
