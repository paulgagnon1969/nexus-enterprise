## CMS PROPOSAL

CMS - Construction Managed Services is a superior construction practice when the size and scope of a project may be too risky for the client and/or the Contractor.

CMS proposals can be cost-plus, cost-plus with bonus structure, or Developer / Partner shared risk solutions; NEXUS CMS differs significantly for the client and the contractor in that CMS allows for:

## CONSTRUCTION RISK-MANAGEMENT

Risk mitigation for both Client and Contractor

### 1. Client Risk
Client risk is generally not the financial risk as most bid projects are bid "At-Risk". The real risk to the client comes in poor quality and potential financial failure of the contractor.

1. Because of these risks, contractors attempt to "Contingency-Bid" whereby they will bid low and use the change order, exclusions, "T's & C's" to make up for expenses that are not accounted for in their bids.

2. This creates the "he-said-she-said" conflict relationship that is all too common.

### 2. Contractor Risk

1. **Financial insolvency** due to Low-Bid, or unclear (vague) scope.

2. **Bid loss** (and costs of bidding) - negative sum game for contractor. If the contractor does win the bid, the negotiation process can be lengthy and either / both parties can pull out if terms are not met to the satisfaction of both parties.

3. **Contractor Remorse** - contractor becomes overwhelmed because they indeed won the proposal, but bid too low to allow for recruitment of additional support (limited resources).

## CMS BENEFITS

Benefits for both parties: CMS proposals align both interest (Client and Contractor) while still retaining the benefit of the outsourcing to the experts the actual construction work.

CMS is a transparent solution where NEXUS will bid the project at fair-market value and begin the "Buy-Down" process to extract any waste from the project to the benefit of the client.

The "Buy-Down" process usually happens after award where the contractor can demonstrate to Subcontractors that they indeed have the award, and they begin the bidding process with subs to ensure their margins are maintained.

NEXUS has a unique process whereby we know what the actual cost is, detail specifically the SOW (Scope of Work), and we tell the sub what we are willing to pay while simultaneously telling the subcontractors what they are being paid for. This virtually eliminates job-site conflict and expedites the construction process.

## WHY CMS VS. TRADITIONAL BID PROCESS?

1. **CMS is transparent** and can save 15-25% or more of proposed bids produced by bidders…

2. **Bid proposal vs. actual…** The Contractors perspective many times is to "BID LOW" and use the "CHANGE ORDER" process to build profits. Client-Contractor Alignment is polar-opposite of Client's needs…

   a. Contractor wants greater margins to cover low-bid initiation
   
   b. Client wants cost savings or high quality.

3. **NEXUS CMS has none of these conflicts.**

   a. There are no Change-order fees. Any and all changes are treated as part of the original SOW
   
   b. Client has complete license to make as many changes as necessary to manage costs and quality.
   
   c. Client sees all transactions, no hidden fees, "kickbacks", or "Buy-Down" profit enhancement tools applied. All savings are passed directly to client.

4. **A "Buy-Down" happens in every job**, the GC secures the bid, then manages margin by utilizing subcontractors that will meet their unpublished post award pricing… this yields **inferior contractors** and **schedule delays**.

## WHY CHOOSE NEXUS CMS VS. TRADITIONAL BID?

NEXUS has chosen to change the paradigm with respect to traditional bid processes.

Bids cost contractors time and money to scope documents, many times the bid process alone can cost tens of thousands of dollars. Factor that across 10-20 contractors and there's productivity costs of $100K - $200K to bid a substantial project even before the project starts.

Bids are "BROUGHT-AND-BOUGHT" with errors because the contractor is resource constrained, and now must dedicate personnel for multiple bids every year, inevitably "Rounding-Errors" occur, or desperation to be the low bidder, or just to under-bid in the hopes of Change Orders or Buy-Downs to improve margins.

NEXUS provides a detailed solution that can be used with any contractor the client decides to use. **You are NOT obligated to use NEXUS as your subcontractor after the SOW is created.**

NEXUS will provide budgets and timelines for subs to follow with detailed cut-sheets, Inventory report, Trade Reports, Productions Reports, and TAM Reports so the client can see what monies go where.

## EXHIBITS AND THEIR PURPOSE

### Exhibit A - Components Report

**Components are an analysis of all:**
- Materials, Equipment, and Labor Components to achieve the following:
- Quantity, Fair Market Unit price, and Totals

Component Reports are used by NEXUS to negotiate with suppliers for "Most Favored Nation Pricing" whereby NEXUS can negotiate down to the number of screws or nails to extract best pricing.

### Exhibit B - Trade Report

Trade Reports provide a detailed itemization of each trade, total hours, material costs.

1. Trade reports are an excellent tool to use to ensure you have no "Resource Constraints" to cause schedule slip.

2. NEXUS uses "Pull Planning" and "Lean Manufacturing methodologies to ensure schedule are tight and projects move smoothly.

3. NEXUS uses "Contractor Prime Strategies" whereby sub contractors will move to the front or rear of the backup-backfill positions (think Second-String). If a subcontractor misses schedules, we promote the second-string to "Prime". All incentives are aligned to ensure subcontractors are top performers.

### Exhibit C - Production Report

1. Production Reports are for subcontractor use, and will be filled out daily. These reports are our quality control to ensure work is completed in accordance with schedule requirements.

2. We provide all reports online and are updated real-time. Client will have ready access to percent complete (% Complete) and action item lists for detailed analysis.

3. Time accounting and PO/Invoicing is completed weekly as progress payments are completely transparent and will be run through NEXUS as we will be the CMS GC.

### Exhibit D - TAM Report (Time and Materials)

1. TAM - Time and Materials Report is for accounting purposes to reconcile actuals vs. budget on a "Per Item" basis.

2. Quite literally the client would have access to budget vs. actual for: Time, Labor Rate, Unit Qty, Unit Price, Market Condition, Total Labor, Labor Burden, Material, Equipment, and Totals.
